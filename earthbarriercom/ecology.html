at Earthbarrier, we're pioneering the field of regenerative ecology. we believe the biosphere is a self-organizing system that has the natural capacity to heal itself, but, just like a patient with a wound, benefits from medical treatment.


the idea of "treatment" captures a broad set of activities that we aim to offer as toolkits to entities interested in building out preemptive responses to climate change, or who want to help reverse it directly. some examples are:


  • deploying trophic layers from the bacterial layer up to restore ecosystems and generate new ones.
  • deploying programmable molecular agents to facilitate ecological interactions.
  • accelerating carbon export to fine-tune ecological stimulation and drive total carbon sequestration.

toward these ambitions, we are developing mathematical models, coupling traditional dynamical systems modeling and state-of-the-art machine learning models, to inform the development and use of our climate technologies. with these models, we aim to describe and predict not only the underlying biological agents, but also the emergent properties that arise from their interactions. with unprecidented volume and resolution of measurements of our climate interventions, we aim to develop models that will reliably predict the complex nature of our planet and the living systems that make it home.
